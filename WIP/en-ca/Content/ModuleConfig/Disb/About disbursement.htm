About disbursement

GCPM provides client-centric tools for claim management: Clients can: 

  • Submit claims and advance requests via a portal.
  • Submit claims and advance requests manually (by email, mail or other means). These can be manually entered in GCPM.
  • View records they have submitted. Any changes, adjustments or reviews are reflected in a disbursement review, and not in submitted claims.

GCPM captures specific invoice and cost-related information with each claim and advance request. Your organization can add fields as required for unique claim review / payment processes.

Claims and advance requests are reviewed to determine payment requirements.

Disbursement reviews

The project is the parent record of disbursement reviews. The project identifies which items are valid in the project budget, what the cash flows are for a given fiscal year, what conditions have to be met for a payment to be made, and which templates to apply for disbursement reviews (based on the associated program).

Key features of disbursement reviews: 

  • Officers create disbursement reviews to validate and process claims and advance requests.
  • Claims can be reviewed individually, or bundled for consolidated review.
  • A business process flow integrates decisions and automates actions for disbursement that are consistent with your organization's review and decision-making processes.
  • Cost items in a single claim can be approved, deleted, deferred, adjusted, or split, to treat one claim with several actions.
    For example, a claim is submitted for an expense, only part of which has been incurred to date. An officer can split the invoice, and recommend the incurred items are paid, and defer the balance to a later time.
  • Deferred items can be included in future disbursement reviews, without the client having to resubmit.

In a disbursement review, officers can:

  • Manage hold back
  • Implement payments or recalculations as the result of amendment to the funding formula on the project.
  • Trigger repayments or receivables required during the active phase of a contribution.
    Note, to be configured locally based on your organization's options in financial integration.
  • Code claim items to specific fiscal years.
  • Indicate if the payment amount should be applied as a previously set-up PAYE.
  • Reconcile all or partial amounts of outstanding advances.
  • Issue new advance amounts.
  • Create negative payments when over-payments or unreconciled advances need to be clawed back.
    Note, to be configured locally based on your organization's options in financial integration.
  • Complete a digital checklist to ensure due diligence on claim review. This disbursement checklist can be tailored to each program, and captures audit trail information data required for claim processing.

GC Client

The associated GC client provides certain controls to manage situations across a client’s entire portfolio. If a client with multiple projects has one project in default, or a recovery is needed, an officer can place a hold on the GC Client record. This hold is identified on all of the GC Client's projects and disbursement reviews, and no payments are possible, until the hold is removed.

Automation
  • Creating a claim or advanced request populated with client data on the portal can be automated.
  • Creating a snapshot document to capture client claims
  • Disbursement reviews are not created automatically.
Design decisions
  • Claims and advance requests are separate records, to allow officers to select records to address in a disbursement review. These decisions may be based on external considerations (e.g. time-sensitive requirement for advance to be issued).
  • Cost and payment calculations was designed in the disbursement review based on claims processing tasks common to each RDA.
  • Your organization can modify default business processes, actions, transitions and business rules for disbursement to reflect organization-specific requirements, such as risk and audit.
  • Certain PAYE features are included in GCPM (e.g. create a PAYE from a disbursement review, indicate a requested payment be charged against a PAYE), but the management / maximization of PAYE is not included.
