Create a client risk assessment

Client risk provides a way to assess a client's financial and management capacity, and is separate from project risk assessment. Client risk assessments typically focus on the client's ability to repay a contribution, its management team capacity, financial situation, competitive position within its key market sectors, and other factors relevant to your organization.

Officers can enter narrative comments such as the rationale for the assessment, mitigation measures or changes to monitoring activities that result from a risk assessment. They can override auto-calculated risk scores, as long as they provide a rationale. Officers can input the next scheduled risk assessment and indicate the frequency of monitoring required to be leveraged in dashboards / workload management.

Client risk assessment is created and associated to a monitoring activity that may include financial statement reviews, site visits, or a review of reports that impact the client's portfolio of projects.

To conduct a client risk assessment:

  1. Open a GC Client record, and navigate to Risk Assessments.

  2. The grid displays risk assessments done to date for this client.

  3. Create a new risk assessment by choosing + ADD NEW RISK ASSESSMENT. The risk assessment form opens. Fill in the fields using onscreen help and the notes below.

    .

    • General - most fields in this section are auto-calculated.
      • Date completed: inserts the date when the Complete risk assessment button is clicked.
    • Assessment
      • Score unchanged: Indicate Yes if the risk rating has changed since the last risk assessment; No if there has been no change.
      • Calculated score: The automatically calculated score, based on the responses to the risk questions / elements.
      • Revised score: Provide an alternative score, if the auto-calculated score is not an accurate portrayal of risk.

      • Rationale: If an alternative score is provided, a rationale is required.

      • Schedule future assessment: Indicate yes to schedule a future risk assessment date.
      • Date of next assessment: a calculated field. Uses the date completed and frequency to determine the date of the next scheduled risk assessment.
    • Risk Category: If risk elements are not used, you can assign values to the risk categories. The overall risk value auto-populates.
    • Risk Element: If risk elements are used, you can assign values to the elements. The category values, and overall risk value, auto-calculates.
  4. Save and close the risk assessment. You can return to a risk assessment at a later time.
  5. When the risk assessment is finalized, click COMPLETE RISK ASSESSMENT.
