Manually delete documents

Documents that are uploaded to a document management system (DMS - e.g. GCDocs or SharePoint) can be manually deleted from the DMS.

Note that deleting a document record in GCPM also deletes the document from the document management system (GCDocs or SharePoint).

To manually delete a document in SharePoint or GCDocs:

  1. Open the record that contains the document you wish to delete.
    The example shows an Account record.
  2. Select the record to delete (or deactivate).
  3. Confirm the deactivation
  4. Close the record.

Best practice: 

  • Deleting vs. Deactivating: 
    • Instead of deleting documents from GCPM, consider deactivating the document instead. This will also delete the document. If your organization does not wish to delete the document on deactivation, you should disable the plugin step that triggers on deactivation of a document record.
  • Deleting documents in GCPM vs. SharePoint or GCDocs
    • It is best to do all of the actions through GCPM. Deleting directly in the DMS will not "break" anything, but users viewing the document record in GCPM may be confused as to why the record is still there, but the document is deleted and the link does not work.
