À propos des flux des processus

Le flux des processus d'affaires (FPA) de  Dynamics 365 est une fonction standard de Dynamics qui est mise à profit dans le GPSC. Les FPA peuvent inclure des décisions, des transitions et des mesures dans le cycle de vie des projets, des débours et d'autres processus communs. Cela aide à assurer une gestion uniforme des documents commerciaux importants.

Un nombre de flux des processus d'affaires déjà configurés sont inclus dans le GPSC. En savoir plus sur les équipes ici : Échantillons de flux de processus d'affaires de la GPSC

Les transitions et les flux constituent le principal moyen de valider les données dans GCPM. Plusieurs exemples de modèles de transition et de flux sont inclus dans la solution.

GPSC Remboursable

  • Le flux de ce programme utilise plusieurs étapes qui permettent au système d’afficher de manière dynamique les données de projet pertinentes en fonction de l’état du projet. Par exemple, à l'étape de la demande reçue, les données requises pour effectuer une évaluation initiale de l'éligibilité sont affichées. Les agents peuvent accéder à d’autres points d’affichage pour afficher les champs de données de l’accord de contribution ou de la revue du déboursement.
  • The transition template for this program models the full project lifecycle, from application received to file closure.
    • It makes extensive use of status, transitions, action sets and decisions.
    • A transition is a possible step between two statuses. Statuses may be used several times in the transition template.
    • For example, Withdrawn is the destination of several transitions in the assessment stage. Clients can withdraw their applications at any status, up to the decision to fund. Therefore for each status, up to Decision to Fund, a transition is required to the Withdrawn status.
    • The transition also contains all possible statuses for a project from application received to project closure. These are the internal status identifiers required for reporting purposes.
    • Your organization may configure unique statuses to reflect your needs. For example, a withdrawn status for initial assessment vs withdrawn from advanced assessment may be needed for reporting purposes.

Ensembles d'actions

    • The GCPM core transition template contains many action sets that lock / unlock fields in the project based on project status.
    • Action sets can capture documents with "moment in time" data (e.g. to verify that certain conditions are met before completing a transition)
    • Action sets can activate features on the project. For example, an amendment cannot be made until a project evaluation is completed; disbursements cannot be created until an agreement is executed.

    • Décisions

    • A decision captures a date stamp (facilitates reporting), and can trigger a decision pathway. For example, multiple recommenders can review a project and move it through approvals until the decision is approved / rejected.
    • This prevents the need to create multiple transitions to capture different levels of approval required for a decision.
    • Decisions are versatile and allow officers to select the path required if multiple authority types are possible. When an officer initiates an Approve assessment decision, they select the appropriate approval path (options include manager & director to full approval by a Deputy Minister). The final user with the right level of approval authority in the chain will complete the transition once they click Approve. All dates and times of each person in the approval chain is captured.
    • Similar examples are include for the approval of a contribution agreement in the GCPM core repayable transition template.

    Amendment process

    • The Amendment transitions template functions much the same way as the project lifecycle one.
    • It is linked to a business process flow, contains transitions between statuses, and has action sets and decisions associated with them.
    • What differs is several possible types of amendments are handled in one transition template. Based on information entered by the officer when creating the amendment, the officer only sees transitions related to the selected amendment type.
    • For example, if they are undertaking an administrative amendment to change internal information on the project, they only see the few transitions related to completing that amendment, instead of the full transition path for a substantive two-way amendment.

    Your organization can view the GCPM transition templates for examples of how to leverage all associated features, to help inform your solution configuration.
