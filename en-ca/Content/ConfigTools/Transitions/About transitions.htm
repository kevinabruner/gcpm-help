About transitions

Transitions - in brief

Transitions is a configuration tool, used in conjunction with business process flows and actions, to manage the life cycle of business records (e.g. project life cycle, disbursement review, etc). Your organization can use transitions to configure when to: record decisions, capture snapshots of data (at a moment in time) , lock records, and many other automated actions.

Risk assessments can be generated as part of the project life cycle, (e.g. integrated into the assessment process) without requiring officers to navigate and undertake risk as a separate activity.

The GCPM XX (name) includes transitions related the negotiation and execution of a CA.

Transitions are used to manage business process flows. Transitions automate some aspects of your business process flows, and change the status on records (e.g. projects, disbursement reviews, applications, contribution agreements, claims, etc.). As users make transitions, data is captured in appropriately configured locations. Each transition captured includes the date and time of the transition (anything else?).

Transitions include:

  • Transition paths contain the logic that defines each pathways in a business process flow. Transition paths can be configured to include validations, decisions, actions and approvals.
  • Transition template: brief description...
  • Status – List of states / codes a component can have at different points of its life cycle. Can be related external status to convey information to the client via the portal.

Design considerations

  • Transitions are linked to a business process flows and bundled in a template that can be applied to projects and associated components through the program profile.
  • RDAs can configure unique business processes for their projects, amendments, claims, etc. by program .
  • Templates can be reused across multiple programs if a generic process is required.

Backlog / future development work

Associated tools

Decisions

  • Transitions can be flagged as decisions. Decisions have additional data and features, for example, a decision can be back-dated to reflect a decision that is taken external to the system. For example, the date a contribution agreement is executed.
  • Associated records and documents can be displayed as part of the decision history. Names and dates of individuals can also be captured as part of a decision process, based on how approval has been configured. link to further help

Authority

  • If a transition requires a decision from a delegated authority, the approval pathway can be configured as part of the transition. For example, a project approval may require a manager, director, director-general, Assistant Deputy Minister, and Deputy Minister recommendation before the Minister’s approval completes the transition.
Document generator - in brief

The document generator can generate any document your organization may need to manage clients and projects. Business users can modify document templates to display CRM data. The document generator is a tool that takes snapshots of data and converts it to human-readable format (e.g. a document, a report or web page). The document generator provides flexible formatting and content design, and can readily tailor documents to reflect the look and feel of your organization.

Document templates can be configured to display project-specific information. Conditional text, such as clauses determine by assessment outcomes and project data, is configured using XML code that is linked to GCPM fields.

A configurator defines the data fields to include in a snapshot, and the snapshot populates an MS Word document template with data in XML format.

GCPM includes a configured CA template.
