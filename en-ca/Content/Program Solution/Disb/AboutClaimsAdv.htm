About claims and advance requests

Claims and advance requests are submitted by clients to pay for expenditures related to their projects. An online form can be configured to collect multiple claims and advance requests, GCPM stores each claim and request as separate records. This permits flexible management of claims, where multiple claims and advance requests can be combined into one disbursement review and payment. Alternatively, several claims and advances submitted at one time can be handled by more than one disbursement review and payment.

  • Claims describe costs incurred by the client. Cost items are displayed in a grid, and include budget category, description, supplier, invoice date, amount and HST/GST. Other fields may be configured to meet your organizational requirements.
  • Advance requestsdescribe what the advance will be expended on. contain one or more advance items. Items are displayed in a grid, and include the budget category, supplier, and monthly-expected cash flow requirement. Other fields may be configured to meet your organizational requirements.

Clients can submit claims and advance requests either online, or manually (by email, mail, fax, etc.). Online submissions are transferred to GCPM, according to portal configuration settings, and a snapshot of the data is captured in a non-editable PDF. The PDF is stored in a location accessible to the client as well as staff at your organization.

Configuring claims and advance forms for GCPM

Claims and advance request forms must be configured separately in GCPM and on a portal. The GCPM forms contain these data: 

  • Administrative information such as the type of submission, client data, date submitted, associated program, etc. This data can be used to set up administrative dashboards for claim / advance / application receipt and registration, and workload management.
  • Unique ID: generated automatically, provides a common reference to both the client and your staff to ensure correct identification of an application.
  • Entity-specifc data Claims forms include cost items, advance requests include advance items.
Configuring forms for the portal

When a client "Submits" an advance request online, data is transferred to GCPM using web services. There are several configuration tasks related to the portal: 

  • Design portal forms using the SmartGuide Designer software. Portal fields are mapped to GCPM fields.
  • Create static or dynamic portal forms, and other options.
  • Modify or build your own web services to transfer data from the portal to GCPM.

Note: 

  • Claims / advance requests submitted online have the status completed.
  • Claims / advance requests submitted manually (e.g. by email, fax, regular mail), must be input manually. These records have the status draft, until manually changed to completed.
  • Only claims / advance requests that are completed will trigger the Disbursement Review business process flow.
Automation
  • A GCPM advance request is auto-populated with data received from portal advance requests according to what has been configured for the portal at your organization.
    Your organization should consider how to manage applications submitted by email, fax or other manual options.
  • Claims, advance requests, applications etc. can be auto-populated with recipient data (e.g. legal name).
  • Minimal auto-population of the information is currently configured. Your organization should determine what information is displayed on forms, and map this to the associated GCPM feature (e.g. claim, advance request, application, etc.).
  • Use the Project ID to auto-register claims and advance requests to a specific project.
    Your organization should consider how to manage claim requests submitted manually
Design considerations

By separating claims and advances into distinct records, your organization has the flexibility to combine multiple claims and / or advance requests into one review / payment.

Associated features / tools

Portal - in brief

The portal is configured to display application forms designed in GCPM to portal users. Portal application forms can be

  • Static: a common set of fields and questions is displayed. In this case, each program / initiative may have a separate application form.
  • Interactive - as users enter information about their project and organization, the data presented to the user dynamically changes. Questions can be tailored based on input from the client

Portal accounts will interact with the GCPM account records. The portal will facilitate the creation of new accounts, and the management of client information, flagging where necessary when requested changes / updates are required.
